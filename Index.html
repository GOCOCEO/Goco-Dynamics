About

Proactive.
Not Reactive.

The social work field trains professionals to handle almost everything — except staying safe when things go sideways. The NASW code of ethics is solid on principles. It is silent on what to do when a client becomes a threat.

GoCo Dynamics fills that gap. Founded by Angelo Coates, MSW candidate and Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu practitioner, GoCo delivers survivability skills built specifically for the helping professions — grounded in the do-no-harm framework and designed for real field situations.

This is not self-defense theater. It is situational awareness, verbal de-escalation, and physical readiness — in that order, every time. The goal is never to fight. The goal is to get home.

GoCo is the professional arm. The mission runs through the Garrison Project, a youth mentorship nonprofit built on the same philosophy: build it before you need it.

The Garrison Project
Mission

The Work
That Matters.

GoCo Dynamics is the revenue engine. The Garrison Project is why it exists.

The Garrison Project is a nonprofit mentorship program built specifically for fatherless young men. Not another talking circle. Not a feelings journal. A structured environment where boys learn to build character, earn leadership, and develop real resilience — on the mat and in the classroom.

The program pairs jiu-jitsu training with concrete life skills: getting an ID, navigating college applications, mock interviews, financial basics, and academic support. Success is measured in grades, attendance, and outcomes — not feelings expressed in a session.

Partner with Us
Movement-Based Resilience Jiu-jitsu teaches leverage, control, and working from bad positions — skills that translate directly off the mat.
Academic Accountability Grade improvement is tracked and celebrated as a concrete outcome. Report cards tell the real story.
